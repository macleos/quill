Event Info

Hello!

Thank you for confirming your spot. Here are some important things that might come in handy before the event:

What we will provide

  • WiFi
  • Meals (lunch and dinner on Saturday, breakfast and lunch on Sunday)
  • Showers
  • Swag
  • Hacking area (with tables and chairs)
  • Sleeping area

What to bring

The Essentials

  • Laptop and charger
  • A UK adaptor (if you are coming from outside of the UK)
  • Valid Government/University ID for admission
  • Towel and toiletries (we have showers and we encourage hackers to use them!)

Optional

  • A blanket (optional, but it might get a bit cold during the night!)
  • Sleeping bag/small pillow
  • Headphones (it might get quite noisy during the event)

When is Hack the Burgh

Registration will close at 11 AM on Saturday, 29 February 2020 and the closing ceremony will end at 5 PM on Sunday, 1 March 2020

Where is Hack the Burgh

Informatics Forum, 10 Crichton Street, Edinburgh EH8 9AB

How do I get to Hack the Burgh

  • From Edinburgh Airport
  • By car (Uber or cab): 27 minutes with an estimated fare of £20-£26
  • By bus
  • Option 1 (41 minutes one way, cost for return: £10.90)
  • Take the Airlink 100 (Return ticket for £7.50) until Haymarket Station (Stop HA)
  • From Haymarket Station, take the no. 2 bus (£1.70 per journey) to The Jewel and stop at Potterrow Station
  • Walk 3 minutes to the Informatics Forum
  • Option 2 (46 minutes one way, cost for return: £7.50)
  • Take the Airlink 100 (Return ticket for £7.50) until Waverley Bridge (Stop WA)
  • Walk 13 minutes to the Informatics Forum
  • We don’t recommend walking the entire way because it will take about 2 hours and 40 minutes!
  • From Edinburgh Waverley Station
  • By car (Uber or cab): 7 minutes with an estimated fare of £3-£4
  • By bus (11 minutes, cost for return: £3.40)
  • Walk 2 minutes to the North Bridge Station (Stop NE) and take the no. 7, 31, 37 bus to Surgeons’ Hall
  • Walk 4 minutes to the Informatics Forum
  • By foot
  • Walk 14 minutes to the Informatics Forum

What if I don’t have a team/my team members did not get accepted?

Part of the fun of a hackathon is meeting new people! We have allocated time at the beginning of the event for a team building activity for everyone to meet and form teams.

Communication during the event

You will receive an invite to join our slack workspace where you can ask questions before, after and during the event, start building your team, join our fun challenges, request for music and so on!

Make sure you follow us on social media to stay updated as the event draws nearer!

I still have more questions…

If there is anything that you’d like to ask or clarify, please do not hesitate to contact us at hello@hacktheburgh.com. If you think you cannot make it to the event anymore, please indicate that on your dashboard so we can give your spot to another enthusiastic hacker!

We hope that the information above is useful. We wish you safe and smooth travels and hope to see you at the event!
